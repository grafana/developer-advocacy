## Summary

In this video, Carl Backquist, a principal engineer at Grafana Labs, and Katia Gelvan from Google's Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) team discuss the recent partnership between Google and Grafana. Katia highlights that the collaboration arose as Google aimed to adopt industry standards, with Grafana's modular architecture and advanced dashboard visualization capabilities making it an ideal choice. She expresses her appreciation for Grafana's diverse widgets that enhance internal monitoring. Carl emphasizes the alignment of SRE culture between Google and Grafana, noting that the SRE book serves as an onboarding resource for new engineers at Grafana, allowing them to integrate Google's experience into their offerings for the community.

Hi, my name is Carl Backquist. I'm a principal engineer at Grafana Labs, and I'm here with Katia Garcia just after our presentation about our partnership between Google and Grafana. 

**Katia, could you tell us a little bit more about that partnership?**

Sure! Thank you, Carl. My name is Katia Gelvan, and I work for SRE Google monitoring in Munich, Germany. I'm excited to share that we started a partnership with Grafana when Google decided to move towards industry standards. Grafana was well positioned for that because of their modular architecture, superior dashboard creation capabilities, and amazing visualization layer.

**What’s your favorite feature in Grafana, Katia?**

For my specific interest, I really appreciate the ability to have all these widgets that can show my internal monitoring in wonderful and more exotic ways; sometimes they're even more useful.

At Grafana Labs, the SRE culture from Google aligns very well with our engineering culture. The SRE book serves as onboarding material for all our new engineers, so we're very happy and proud to take the experience from Google and integrate it into Grafana, making it available for the whole community.

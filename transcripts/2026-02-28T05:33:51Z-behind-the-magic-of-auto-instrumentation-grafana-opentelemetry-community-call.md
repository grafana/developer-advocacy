# Behind the magic of auto-instrumentation (Grafana ❤️‍🔥 OpenTelemetry Community Call)

Published on 2026-02-28T05:33:51Z

## Description

You add the OpenTelemetry Java agent, restart your app - and like magic, observability appears. But is it really magic?

What’s actually enabled by default? What telemetry should you expect to see? What’s missing? And what might you want to tweak, tune, or even turn off?

In this episode, we go behind the magic of OpenTelemetry auto-instrumentation and explore how to navigate its rapidly growing list of instrumentations. Jay DeLuca (OpenTelemetry Java Instrumentation & Contrib Approver and Ecosystem Explorer Project Lead) joins us to unpack how it all works under the hood - and how to approach that “magic” with informed curiosity.

We’ll cover:
- What really happens when you drop in the OTel Java agent - and what you get out of the box
- How to determine what’s actually instrumented, what telemetry is emitted, and how stable those instrumentations are
- How we’re making OTel easier to navigate with the Ecosystem Explorer
- Where and how you can jump in and contribute to OpenTelemetry

Join the conversation, bring your questions, and if it sparks something for you, jump in and help us build and improve OpenTelemetry together.

## Summary

In the Grafana Labs OpenTelemetry Community Call, hosted by Luda Malova, Jay De Lucro, a software engineer and former site reliability engineer, discussed the intricacies of OpenTelemetry and its auto-instrumentation capabilities. He shared his unconventional journey into the world of observability, detailing how he transitioned from a software engineer to a site reliability engineer and became involved with OpenTelemetry. The conversation covered the workings of the Java agent, which utilizes bytecode manipulation to provide automatic instrumentation, allowing developers to gather metrics and traces without extensive manual coding. Jay emphasized the importance of semantic conventions for consistency across different systems and showcased the new Ecosystem Explorer project, aimed at improving documentation and discoverability of instrumentation details. The discussion also addressed the challenges of integrating OpenTelemetry with various frameworks, including Spring and the necessity for custom spans. The call concluded with an invitation for community engagement and contributions to OpenTelemetry, particularly for the Ecosystem Explorer project.
